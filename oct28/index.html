My Country My Vote

About the Action

This October 28th, ahead of the GOP debate at CU Boulder, Movimiento Estudiantil Chican@ de Aztlán at CU-Boulder (MEChA) and Pi Lambda Chi Latina Sorority will be sponsoring MY COUNTRY, MY VOTE, a rally that will commence an unprecedented 12-month voter registration campaign to mobilize Colorado’s Latino, immigrant, and allied voters. Join us as Latino leaders, Latino organizations, immigration reform supporters, and allies gather in Farrand Field to express a vociferous rejection of the anti-immigrant rhetoric that has dominated this electoral cycle.

In addition to demonstrating the political power of one of the country's fastest growing demographics, leaders will also call on the next President and Congress make the passage of a fair and comprehensive immigration reform a priority during the first 100 days of the next presidential term.

We are proud to sponsor an event that takes a stand for immigrant rights at the same time that it promotes civic engagement. In addition, this event will feature top Latino and immigration leaders, including Katherine Archuleta, Former Director US Office of Personnel Management and 2012 Political Director, Obama for America; Dolores Huerta, Co-Founder of the United Farm Workers Union and Longtime Civil Rights and Labor Activist; Federico Peña, former Mayor of Denver and a former US Secretary of Transportation and Energy; and Ken Salazar, Former US Senator and US Secretary of the Interior. Entravision and Radio TriColor are also bringing their #1 show, ERAZNO Y CHOKOLATA, to broadcast live from Farrand Field from 2-7pm MT.

The “My Country, My Vote” campaign is a multi-group effort led by Federico Peña; CIRC Action Fund; Colorado Latino Leadership Advocacy and Research Organization; Colorado Progressive Coalition; Escuela Tiatelolco; LACLAA; Mi Familia Vota; MEChA; Pi Lambda Chi Latina Sorority; Rights For All People; SEIU Local 105; Servicios De La Raza; and Together Colorado.
